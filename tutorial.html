Tutorial

Hi! So you have decided to help the ForestWatchers Project. Great!!!

Your participation is appreciated. We hope that your contribution may help us to build a forest map and protect this particular area.

To do that, you will verify the automatic classification done by an Artificial Neural Network (ANN).

Let's see a little bit more how can you do that!

The goal is to correct the automatic classification, if necessary.

In the main interface you will see two images, side-by-side. On left side you have the satellite image and on the right you have the automatic classification of the first image. The classification is done in two classes: FOREST and NON-FOREST.

In both images you will see a square marking the area you are supposed to correct (the red square in the satellite image and the blue square in the classification image). Both squares represents the exactelly same image.

If your browser supports the necessary technology, you can try to adjust the brightness and the contrast of the satellite image to obtain a better view.

Tip: Your adjusts will be maintained while you don't close or update the page.

You can also zoom in or zoom out the images to obtain a better context of the area.

Look for the plus and minus signs on the top left corner of the images.

Tip: When you change the zoom of one image, the other will also be adjusted automatically.

Once you have made up your mind, click in either the FOREST or the NON-FOREST buttom below the classification image to pre-register your contribution.

Tip: Depending on your selection, the color of the square in the classification image will change.

To finalize the task and register you contribution in the ForestWatchers servers, just click Submit.

Those are the basic steps! Now we only need your help to safe the forests.

Remember, whenever you want to remember something else, just click in the Tutorial button on the top of the images.

So, let's start? :-)
